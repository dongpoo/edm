39TH Annual SABCS Highlights
 
PLENARY
LECTURE 1
 
The 39th Annual San Antonio Breast Cancer Symposium began with an overview of the management of metastatic ER+ breast cancer by Dr. Stephen R.D. Johnston of The Royal Marsden Hospital & Institute of Cancer Research, London, United Kingdom. Dr. Johnston presented an overview of therapies to improve the efficacy of aromatase inhibitors (AIs) as the standard of care in first-line therapy. This can be accomplished through the addition of new agents, such as fulvestrant, gefitinib, and selective CDK 4/6 inhibitors, specifically palbociclib and ribociclib. Second-line treatment options, including fulvestrant and exemestane, were also discussed, as well as PI3K, TORC, and AKT inhibitors currently in clinical development. The optimal sequence for these therapies was also discussed, including considerations such as tumour biology, clinical features of the disease, and patient factors, all of which should be considered when choosing the most appropriate therapy for patients.

PLENARY
LECTURE 2
 
The second day of the 39th Annual San Antonio Breast Cancer Symposium began with a Plenary Lecture by Dr. Ben Ho Park of the Sidney Kimmel Comprehensive Cancer Centre at Johns Hopkins, Baltimore, Maryland entitled "Cell-free plasma tumour DNA in breast oncology". Circulating cell-free DNA (cfDNA) are small DNA fragments shed by all cells that are present in the circulation. Circulating tumour DNA (ctDNA) refers to DNA specifically from tumour cells. Plasma tumour DNAs (ptDNA) are ctDNAs found in plasma, and can be detected using digital PCR and high-throughput next-generation sequencing (NGS). In patients with metastatic breast cancer, mutations can be reliably detected in ptDNA using NGS to help determine the extent of disease and if a patient has micrometastases that may require modification of a treatment regimen.
