Identification and management of familial hypercholesterolaemia and the role of PCSK9 inhibition - Assoc. Professor David Sullivan
Familial hypercholesterolaemia (FH) is the most common potentially fatal genetic condition and is grossly underdiagnosed in Australia and many other countries. While FH does have a poor prognosis, early identification and treatment has the potential to alter the course of the disease. The novel anti-PCSK9 antibodies represent a new treatment paradigm for FH patients. These provide another mechanism for cholesterol lowering – different from and complementary to existing medications.
