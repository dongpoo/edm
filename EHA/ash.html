  MEET THE HAEMLIVE EXPERTS
 
Dr George Grigoriadis
Dr George Grigoriadis currently holds a position as Clinical and Laboratory Haematologist at Monash Health and The Alfred Hospital with a senior lecturer appointment in the Department of Medicine, School of Clinical Sciences at Monash Health.

Most recently, Dr Grigoriadis has been awarded a Victorian Cancer Agency Clinical Research Fellowship over 4 years to determine how inflammation may be an enabling characteristic in the evolution of MDS. As the head of the myeloma service at Monash Health/MHTP, Grigoriadis has expanded the program and increased its profile as a basic sciences, translational and clinical trials site.
 
Dr Adam Bryant
Dr Adam Bryant trained in haematology at St Vincent's Hospital in Sydney, having originally completed medical school at the University of Queensland. He went on to do his PhD studies under the supervision of Professor David Ma at the AMR in Darlinghurst. This was a molecular biology basic science project entitled "The Role of MicroRNAs in Acute Myeloid Leukaemia" and was awarded in late 2012.

While writing up his PhD, Bryant undertook a clinical fellowship at the Vancouver General Hospital in Canada as the Leukaemia and BMT fellow. Adam has been a staff specialist at Liverpool Hospital since May 2012 and a development member of the allogeneic BMT program which commenced at this time. He sub-specialises in malignant haematology with a specific interest in stem cell transplantation. He is co-chair of the rapidly expanding Liverpool Haematology Clinical Trials program and is currently serving as the national Chairperson of the EviQ Haematology Reference Committee (taking up this post in September 2015).
