COE Initiative Focus:
Early Experience Team Account Planning roll-out in South Africa

By Hendrik Van Staden and Shariq Bukhari

The South African Account Planning training meeting took place in January and was a huge success.

Some of the major objectives of the meeting included role clarity and responsibilities, setting out the importance of the RADL approach to customer interaction and prioritization, and highlighting how specific activities and customer touch points can create value based on market insights. The intelligence captured in this model will give the sales team the data and information needed to better understand our customers, prioritize their time more efficiently and plan differently.

Highlights:

  • We looked at the business holistically, in conjunction with the TD, Customer Care and Marketing teams, to develop touchpoint programs and activities tied to the RADL model for the remainder of the calendar year 2017.
  • The SFE team also conducted an effective Change Management training session to enable the teams to better embrace the new Account Planning process.
  • Quarterly reviews, regular feedback loop sessions, and training are being scheduled with team managers and initiative champions on an ongoing basis throughout 2017.

The RADL model introduces a consistent approach to account prioritization, not just for the Sales team within South Africa, but for the Sales teams within the five other Early Experience Teams (EETs) around the globe.

The insights and data gained by all the EETs can be more easily shared with other functions within the organization. Eventually these insights will be utilized to enable the Commercial business to roll out this model on a global scale.

Please take a few minutes to watch our videos, made by members of the South Africa Sales team, for their feedback on the RADL approach. For more information on the Account Planning initiative, please contact Shariq Bukhari: shariq.bukhari@monsanto.com

COE Initiative Focus:
DART 1.0 (Revenue & Product Optimization)

By Tom Breen and Cornie Thiessen

What is DART 1.0?

DART 1.0 is the first version of the global discretionary and rebate tool developed to manage the write-up and approval of market funding programs. Over the coming months, every Hub will migrate to DART from their current market funding systems.

What is initially being deployed?

In this first deployment, we gain access to a global standard process and IT platform for the development and approval of our marketing programs. All existing approval and program compliance features will migrate to DART 1.0 to preserve legal and compliance controls.

During November, several of our AA Hub Brand Managers were the first group in the world to be trained on DART and are now at the global forefront in putting the tool to use. The Global Development team are currently in the process of retrospectively loading many of our FY17 YTD marketing programs into DART, and system access has been assigned to our Marketing Program stakeholders within the hub.

RPO will be reaching out to marketing program developers over the next few weeks for a refresh on the write up and approval process so we can begin to capture all Q3 and Q4 programs in DART. We eagerly await your feedback and welcome additional design ideas to improve the system.

We would also like to recognise and thank Pieter Taljaard for representing our hub on the global design team.
